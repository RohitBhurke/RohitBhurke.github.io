Most analysts work with data from a distance. I built solutions standing next to the machines. These are two problems I identified, solved, and measured — before I even had the title.

01
+64% Efficiency

The Punching Machine Bottleneck

The Problem

In the conductor fabrication section, our high-tonnage punching machine was only punching 2 holes at a time. Operators had to manually add a stopper and rotate the busbar between each punch — consuming excessive cycle time and becoming a clear production bottleneck.

What I Did

I talked directly with the operators, studied the machine's capabilities and die geometry, and proposed manufacturing a new custom die that could punch 5 holes simultaneously — eliminating the rotation and stopper steps entirely.

The Result

The new die was manufactured and deployed. Cycle time dropped dramatically — punching efficiency increased by 64%. The same output now required significantly less time and operator intervention.

2→5 Holes per punch
64% Efficiency gain
0 Rotation steps
02
+47% Faster

The Masking Waste Problem

The Problem

Before and after painting busbars and conductors, the contact surface areas had to be masked to protect them. The existing process required multiple layers of masking tape — applied before painting, then fully removed before assembly. This created double handling, material waste, and significant time loss.

What I Did

I analysed the masking workflow and proposed replacing the multi-layer tape process with custom rubber silicone sleeves that could be directly inserted over the contact surfaces. Only a single layer of masking is now required, and the sleeves are reusable.

The Result

Process speed increased by 47%. Material costs dropped due to reusable sleeves. The freed-up manpower was redeployed to other sections. Paint quality on contact surfaces also improved due to cleaner masking edges.

47% Faster process
1 Layer needed
↑ Quality improved
